Where Affirm customers are escalating — and what the patterns reveal

An analysis of 15,322 CFPB complaints filed against Affirm from 2021 to 2026 — surfacing the servicing categories where customers are not finding resolution through existing channels.

15,322 verified complaints · 8 complaint categories identified · 12× growth 2021–2025 · Source: CFPB public database · Through June 2026
Context
What a CFPB complaint tells us

A CFPB complaint is filed after a customer has already attempted to resolve their issue directly with Affirm and felt the outcome was unsatisfactory. The data does not tell us what happened inside Affirm's support process — but it does tell us which complaint types are consistently resulting in customers reaching for an external escalation path. That pattern is the signal.

How to read this analysis: CFPB complaints represent the subset of Affirm's servicing interactions where customers escalated to a federal regulator. They do not represent all servicing contacts — and Affirm's overall CSAT is not captured here. What this data does reveal is the shape of unresolved frustration: which issue types are consistently generating escalations, and how that mix is changing over time.

Methodology
How this was built
①
Data source

Full CFPB Consumer Complaint Database (16M+ rows). Filtered to all records where Company = "Affirm" (case-insensitive). Result: 15,322 complaints. No manual curation — every matching record included.

②
Category mapping

CFPB uses ~20 issue codes. These were mapped to 8 customer-facing theme buckets based on the underlying customer experience, not the legal/regulatory framing. Sub-issues provide further granularity within each bucket.

③
Limitations

CFPB complaints represent escalated cases only — not Affirm's broader servicing volume or CSAT. This analysis is about escalation patterns, not overall servicing performance. Complaint categorization is approximate.
